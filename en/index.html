Interactive Violin Fingerboard Chart for Scales and Positions

This violin fingerboard chart helps players see where notes, scales, and positions sit on the fingerboard. Choose a key, switch between major and minor, and select a position to highlight the notes that matter for your practice session.

What You Can Use It For

Use the chart to learn violin notes, review scale patterns, compare keys, and connect finger placement with music theory. It is especially useful when you are practicing first position, moving into higher positions, or trying to understand how a key signature changes the notes on each string.

How to Read the Fingerboard

The strings are shown as G, D, A, and E. Notes in the selected scale are marked directly on the fingerboard, while the highlighted area shows the selected position. The tonic note is styled differently so you can quickly find the home note of the key.

How to Practice With It

Start with one key, such as C major or G major, and play slowly from the lowest marked note to the highest marked note. Then change the position filter and notice which notes stay familiar and which notes require a new hand shape. This makes scale practice more visual and less dependent on memorizing isolated finger numbers.

Circle of Fifths Reference

The circle of fifths helps you move between related keys and understand sharps, flats, and relative minor keys. Click a key in the circle to update the fingerboard and see the matching scale layout immediately.

Violin Fingerboard Chart FAQ

Is this chart for beginners?

Yes. Beginners can use it to learn note names on the violin fingerboard, while intermediate players can use it to review scales, positions, and key signatures.

Does it show violin positions?

Yes. You can select a position and the chart will highlight the notes that fit that position range, making it easier to connect scale notes with left-hand placement.

Can I use it for minor scales?

Yes. Switch the mode control from major to minor, then choose the tonic to see the minor scale pattern on the fingerboard.
